# Miami Inspiration References (Feb 19, 2026)

Curated upscale Miami restaurant websites worth emulating for the La Costa redesign.

## 1. The Surf Club Restaurant — Thomas Keller  
**URL:** https://www.surfclubrestaurant.com/  
**Why it works:**  
- Minimal, high-contrast hero with serif headlines and ample breathing room telegraphs quiet luxury.  
- Story-driven copy (“Celebrate decades of leisure time…”) reinforces heritage without crowding the layout.  
- Immaculate still-life photography (True Ribeye, Lemon Meringue Tart) acts as anchor visuals instead of busy galleries.  
- Reservation CTA is modest but persistent; typography mix feels straight out of a vintage yacht club invitation.  
**Takeaways for La Costa:** Pair a restrained serif/sans duo with oversized white space, and anchor each section with one art-directed photo instead of grids of stock shots.

## 2. Carbone Miami — Major Food Group  
**URL:** https://carbonemiami.com/  
**Why it works:**  
- Deep terracotta + emerald palette over a jet-black backdrop feels decadent and cinematic.  
- Section headings (“CLASSIC,” “PATIO,” “DESIGN”) read like magazine spreads; every block keeps to 3–4 sentence micro-copy.  
- Design notes highlight textures (damask walls, Murano sconces) which sells the experience as much as the food.  
- Newsletter + press links sit inside a clean column, proving utility items can still look premium.  
**Takeaways:** Use rich accent colors surgically (buttons, dividers, pull quotes) against muted sand/ivory backgrounds to keep the vibe upscale coastal instead of tropical tourist.

## 3. Mandolin Aegean Bistro  
**URL:** https://www.mandolinrestaurant.com/  
**Why it works:**  
- Endless airy photography carousel sells the sun-drenched patio without a single paragraph.  
- Copy pillars (“simplicity / authenticity / quality”) double as visual separators—short, confident language.  
- Button styles are ghost/lightweight, keeping focus on imagery and whitespace.  
- Palette is straight Cycladic blue + chalk white, instantly recognizable.  
**Takeaways:** Let photography drive the narrative and keep CTAs featherweight. For La Costa, we need a curated photo set (hero, tablescape, chef portrait, cocktail).

## 4. La Mar Miami by Gastón Acurio  
**URL:** https://www.lamarcebicheria.com/miami/  
**Why it works:**  
- Continually updated news/events feed shows the restaurant as a living brand, not a static brochure.  
- Chef storytelling (Diego Oka profile) humanizes the experience and justifies premium pricing.  
- Content hierarchy blends menu overview, private events, and press hits without overwhelming the viewer.  
**Takeaways:** Add a “Chef’s Notes” or “From the Kitchen” block plus a concise events blurb so La Costa looks active year-round.

### Design Language Summary
- **Tone:** Heritage coastal—think Surf Club polish with Mandolin breeziness.  
- **Colors:** Deep ocean blue (#0B2C3F), sand (#F0E6D8), champagne gold accents (#C7A770), charcoal typography.  
- **Type:** Serif display (Cormorant Garamond or Canela) for headlines, clean sans (Inter/Neue Haas) for body.  
- **Imagery Needs:** Twilight exterior, plated seafood macro, cocktail close-up, dining room vignette, chef portrait, detail shot (linen, tableware).  
- **Interactions:** Slow parallax on hero, fade-in sections, subtle underline animation on nav hover, scroll-triggered typography reveals.  

Use these references as the guardrails for the La Costa rebuild—every section should feel like it could live on The Surf Club site but with Mandolin’s warmth.
